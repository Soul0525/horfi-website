ABOUT US

The Compassionate Community for the Underprivileged

House of Refuge began when a group of fervent individuals encountered street children who had been abandoned, neglected or maltreated by their own families. Prodded by a heartfelt resolve, this auspicious event birthed a halfway house in September 21, 1986, which gave shelter and provided the basic needs for street children. The halfway house was named Bahay Kanlungan in 1990.

In the following year, it became a licensed child-caring institution, which adopted the name House of Refuge Foundation, Inc. The HORFI has gone a long way from the first batch of 7 street children in 1986. Since its inception, House of Refuge has served more than 1000 children and currently cares for 37 children ages ranging from 4 to 17 years old.

Frequently Asked Questions

1. What does House of Refuge Foundation do?

We provide a safe home, care, education, and spiritual nurture for children who have been abandoned, neglected, or abused. We support their holistic development emotionally, physically, socially, and spiritually while working toward meaningful, long-term outcomes.

2. Who can receive support from House of Refuge?

Our programs are designed for children who are in vulnerable situations, such as those who have been abandoned, neglected, surrendered, or otherwise need a loving place of care and stability.

3. How long has House of Refuge been operating?

House of Refuge began in 1986 with a small group of children and volunteers. Over the decades, we have grown into a licensed child-caring institution serving many children and families with quality programs.

4. What makes your foundation different from others?

We are a faith-based institution rooted in compassion and service to the most vulnerable children. Our holistic approach includes nurturing their faith, fostering educational and personal development, and equipping them for a hopeful future.

5. How can I get involved or support the foundation?

You can support us by: Donating to help with care, programs, and daily needs, Volunteering your time and skills, Partnering with us through community and academic collaborations. Visit our Donate or Volunteer page for details.
